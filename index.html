About Me

William ("Will") Hicky is a West Tennessee native, having been born and raised in Memphis. He graduated from the University of Mississippi in 2003 with a Bachelor

of Business Administration degree, where he was a member of the National Society of Collegiate Scholars and on the Chancellor’s Honor Roll. William also received his law degree from the University of Mississippi School of Law in 2006.

William is admitted to practice in every State and Federal Court of Tennessee, including the Federal Bankruptcy Courts, 6th Circuit Court of Appeals, and Supreme Court of the United States.

Mr. Hicky Law focuses its practice in the areas of business & contract litigation, debt collection & negotiation, corporate fraud recovery as well as personal injury, car & tractor trailer wrecks, premises liability, and workers’ compensation. He has received an AV rating through Martindale-Hubbell's peer review for highest ethical standards and legal ability as well as being named a Super Lawyer for every year since 2016.

William has been recognized and regarded by his clients, his peers in the legal profession, and judges as highly competent, ethical, and courteous but zealous in the protection and advancement of his clients’ interests.

William is a member of the Tennessee and Nashville Bar Associations where he served as a Board Member on both Young Lawyers Divisions of the Nashville Bar Association and Tennessee Bar Association. He is also a member of the Harry J. Phillips American Inn of Courts as well as on the Board of Directors of the Nashville Sports Council.

Prior to opening Hicky Law, William was a senior associate at several Nashville firms where he concentrated in the areas of personal injury and workers’ compensation in addition to representing numerous financial institutions in debt collection and bankruptcy proceedings as well as business litigation and insurance defense.

William has been long-active in the Nashville philanthropic community and has been named to the Tennessee Bar Association's Leadership Law Class Nashville Bar Association's Leadership Forum, Young Leaders Council Class 68, and one of Nashville’s Top 30 Under 30 by the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation. He was recently inducted as a Fellow into the Nashville Bar Foundation.
